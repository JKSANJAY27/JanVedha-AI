# Chief Minister's Thayumanavar Scheme

## Overview
The Chief Minister's Thayumanavar Scheme is a flagship poverty alleviation initiative by the Government of Tamil Nadu launched in 2024. It aims to completely eradicate systemic poverty by identifying the poorest 5 lakh families and progressively improving their livelihoods over two years through an integrated, saturated delivery of all government welfare schemes.

## Eligibility Criteria
- **Target Audience:** The poorest and most marginalized families in Tamil Nadu.
- **Identification:** Beneficiaries are identified through rigorous baseline assessments based on factors such as deep economic distress, structural vulnerability, lack of essential resources, health issues, and destitution.
- **Focus Groups include:**
  - Destitute windows and elderly individuals living alone without care.
  - Families facing continuous health crises, chronic illnesses, or severe disabilities.
  - Single-parent households facing severe economic difficulties.
  - Landless agricultural laborers, specially those belonging to SC/ST communities.
  - Nomadic/Denotified communities lacking stable homes and incomes.
  - Families without pucca housing, unable to meet basic daily food requirements.

## Required Documents
- Smart Ration Card (Family Card)
- Aadhaar Card
- Supporting documents based on specific vulnerability (e.g., widow certificate, disability certificate, medical records).

## Application Process
1. Gram Sabhas and local governing bodies proactively identify these highly vulnerable families based on specific indicators.
2. Verified beneficiaries are enrolled via grassroots administrative mapping.
3. Instead of the citizen discovering the schemes, the administration delivers a 'Convergence Package', actively clustering benefits like free housing, old-age pensions, MGNREGS job cards, Urimai Thogai, health cover, and skill training simultaneously to the family.

## Contact / Portal
- **Local Contact:** Gram Panchayat Office / Local BDO / VPRC (Village Poverty Reduction Committee).
- Managed under the Tamil Nadu State Rural Livelihoods Mission (TNSRLM).

## Financial Benefit
- No direct lump sum is given. Instead, families receive comprehensive poverty alleviation support combining benefits worth tens of thousands of rupees via unified implementation of existing housing, pension, health, education, and livelihood generation schemes.
