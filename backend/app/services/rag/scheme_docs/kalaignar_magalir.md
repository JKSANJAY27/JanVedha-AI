# Kalaignar Magalir Urimai Thogai Scheme

## Overview
The Kalaignar Magalir Urimai Thogai Scheme is a flagship welfare program launched by the Government of Tamil Nadu to recognize the uncompensated labor of women in households, elevate their livelihood, and improve their standard of living. It provides a monthly financial assistance of ₹1,000 to eligible women heads of families.

## Eligibility Criteria
- **Family Head:** The applicant must be a woman identified as the head of the family on the ration card. If a man is listed as the head, his wife is considered the eligible woman head for this scheme. Unmarried women, widows, and transwomen heading families are also eligible.
- **Age Limit:** The applicant must have completed 21 years of age.
- **Economic Criteria (Income & Wealth limits):**
  - **Income Restriction:** The annual family income must be less than ₹2,50,000 (Two Lakh Fifty Thousand Rupees).
  - **Land Ownership:** The family must not own more than 5 acres of wetland or 10 acres of dryland.
  - **Vehicle Ownership:** No family member should own a four-wheeler for personal use (tractors and commercial vehicles are exempted).
  - **Electricity Consumption:** Annual household electricity consumption must be less than 3,600 units.
- **Exclusions:**
  - Income tax payers.
  - Families where members are State/Central Government employees, PSU employees, or bank employees.
  - Elected representatives (Members of Parliament/Legislative Assembly, Panchayat Presidents, etc.).
  - Beneficiaries receiving regular social security pensions like Old Age Pension, Widow Pension, or Unmarried Women Pension (Note: Families having members receiving disabled pension ARE still eligible).

## Required Documents
- Smart Ration Card (Family Card)
- Aadhaar Card of the applicant
- Bank Passbook (Aadhaar-seeded bank account)
- Electricity Bill/Consumer Number

## Application Process
1. The government conducts special camps at the Fair Price Shop (Ration Shop) level.
2. Applications are distributed at the ration shops in advance.
3. Applicants fill out the primary application and self-declaration form.
4. Applicants submit the form at the designated camp with their Aadhaar card, ration card, electricity bill, and bank passbook.
5. A biometric/OTP authentication is done to link the application.
6. Verification is done electronically against government databases (EB, Income Tax, Transport module) and via field verification if necessary.

## Contact / Portal
- **State Portal:** https://kmut.tn.gov.in
- **Grievance Portal:** E-Sevai Centres / RDO (Revenue Divisional Officer)
- **Local Contact:** Ration Shop (Fair Price Shop) / Village Administrative Officer (VAO).

## Financial Benefit
- Direct Bank Transfer (DBT) of ₹1,000 every month sent directly to the Aadhaar-seeded bank account of the selected beneficiary.
