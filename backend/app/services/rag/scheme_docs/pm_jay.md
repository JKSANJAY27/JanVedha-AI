# Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB PM-JAY)

## Overview
Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY is the world's largest health assurance scheme fully financed by the government. It provides a health cover of ₹5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization across public and empaneled private hospitals in India.

## Eligibility Criteria
The scheme aims to target poor, deprived rural families and identified occupational categories of urban workers' families.

- **Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 Categories:** Families falling under specific deprivation criteria D1, D2, D3, D4, D5, and D7 of SECC 2011 are eligible.
- **Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY):** Active RSBY beneficiary families as of February 28, 2018.
- **Senior Citizens Expansion (2024):** All senior citizens aged 70 years and above, irrespective of their socio-economic status, income, or existing health insurance, are eligible.
- **Family Details:** There is no restriction on family size, age, or gender.
- **Pre-existing Conditions:** All pre-existing diseases are covered from day one.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar Card (Mandatory for generating Ayushman Card)
- Ration Card (for establishing family relationships)
- Mobile Number (for OTP and communication)
- PM-JAY Family ID (if already identified in the SECC list)

## Application Process
AB PM-JAY is an entitlement-based scheme. There is no formal enrolment process. Beneficiaries must verify their identity using Aadhaar at an empaneled hospital or Common Service Centre (CSC) to generate their "Ayushman Card" (e-card).

1. Check eligibility on `mera.pmjay.gov.in` or via the Ayushman App using Aadhaar or Ration Card number.
2. Visit an empaneled hospital or nearest CSC to complete e-KYC.
3. Once approved, the PM-JAY e-card is issued.

## Contact / Portal
- **Central Portal:** https://mera.pmjay.gov.in
- **Toll-Free Helpline:** 14555
- **Local Contact:** Ayushman Mitra at any empaneled government/private hospital.

## Financial Benefit
Provides cashless medical coverage up to ₹5,00,000 per family per year for inpatient (hospitalization) treatment, including pre-hospitalization (3 days) and post-hospitalization (15 days) expenses like diagnostics and medicines.
