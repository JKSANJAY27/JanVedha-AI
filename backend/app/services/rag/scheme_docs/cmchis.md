# Chief Minister's Comprehensive Health Insurance Scheme (CMCHIS) - Tamil Nadu

## Overview
CMCHIS is a cashless health insurance scheme launched by the Government of Tamil Nadu. It provides quality healthcare to eligible persons through empaneled government and private hospitals to reduce their out-of-pocket healthcare expenses. The scheme incorporates the PM-JAY benefits in Tamil Nadu.

## Eligibility Criteria
- **Residency:** Only residents of Tamil Nadu are eligible.
- **Family Income:** The annual family income must be less than ₹1,20,000 (One Lakh Twenty Thousand Rupees).
- **Inclusion Criteria:** 
  - Families holding a Smart Ration Card from Tamil Nadu.
  - Sri Lankan refugees living in camps.
  - Migrant workers holding valid labor cards.
  - Orphans or dependent children in registered orphanages.
- **Government Employees:** State and Central Government employees/pensioners, who have their own health schemes, are generally not eligible.

## Required Documents
- Original Smart Ration Card (Income is cross-verified via the Public Distribution System database)
- Income Certificate from VAO/Tahsildar (if income is not printed or verifiable via Ration Card)
- Aadhaar Card (for all adult members)
- Photograph (taken at the kiosk during enrollment)

## Application Process
1. Eligible families must carry their Smart Ration Card and Aadhaar card to the designated e-kiosk at the local District Collectorate or specified Government Hospital.
2. An official will verify the documents and family income.
3. Biometric data and photographs of all family members will be captured.
4. An e-card (CMCHIS ID Card) will be generated and issued on the spot or sent later.
5. In emergencies, hospitals can generate temporary cards for eligible unconsolidated beneficiaries using their Ration Card/Aadhaar.

## Contact / Portal
- **State Portal:** https://www.cmchistn.com
- **Toll-Free Helpline:** 104
- **Local Contact:** CMCHIS liaison officer at any empaneled government or private hospital.

## Financial Benefit
- Provides cashless coverage of up to ₹5,00,000 per family, per year for specific listed procedures, surgeries, and medical treatments.
- Covers diagnostic procedures, pre-existing conditions, follow-up care, and maternity.
